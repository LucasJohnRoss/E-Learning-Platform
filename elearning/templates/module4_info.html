Secure Payments

Secure payment practices help protect both individuals and organisations from fraud, scams, and financial loss. Attackers often target online payments by stealing card details, redirecting transactions, or tricking people into approving fraudulent payments. Understanding how to verify payment requests and protect financial information is essential in any workplace.

Verify Payment Requests

Always confirm the source of a payment request before taking action. Criminals may impersonate managers, suppliers, or finance teams and ask for urgent payments or changes to bank details. Never rely on email alone always double-check through an approved communication channel such as a phone call or internal system.

Use Trusted Payment Platforms

Payments should only be made through verified and trusted systems. Avoid entering financial information on unfamiliar websites or platforms that lack security indicators such as HTTPS. Unsecured or unofficial sites increase the risk of card theft or unauthorised access.

Watch for Refund and Invoice Scams

Attackers often send fake invoices or issue false refund notices to trick users into submitting card details or confirming payments. Unexpected invoices, duplicate requests, or unrecognised vendors should always be reported or checked with your finance department.

Card Security

Card details including full number, expiry date, and CVV must never be shared over email, messaging apps, or plain text. These details should only be entered into secure, approved systems. Even images or screenshots of card details can be enough for attackers to commit fraud.

How to Stay Safe When Making Payments

  • Verify payment requests through a trusted communication channel.
  • Only enter payment information on secure, approved websites.
  • Watch for unexpected invoices, refund requests, or urgent emails.
  • Never share card information through unprotected channels.
  • Report suspicious payment activity to finance or IT immediately.
